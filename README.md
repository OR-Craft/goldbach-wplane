# goldbach-wplane

Computational observations on the complex-plane anatomy of Goldbach major/minor arc decompositions.

Overview
This repository contains the source code, data, and manuscript for an empirical study of the signed major and minor arc contributions in a discrete-grid decomposition of the von Mangoldt weighted Goldbach convolution at scales up to 10^9.
The principal finding is a moderate positive correlation (r ≈ +0.67, converged) between the signed major and minor arc contributions, differing in sign from all mechanical coupling models tested. A complex diagnostic w(N) = M(N) + i·m(N) reveals tight angular concentration, modular family structure, and data consistent with a crossover from a major-dominated to a cancellation-dominated regime.

The study identified and corrected a measurement artefact (aperture amplification at narrow arc neighbourhoods) during the investigation. All findings are purely empirical and no claim regarding the Goldbach conjecture is made.

Provenance

Final reported results are generated from the converged wide-arc pipeline (Final/goldbach_wide_arc.py) and its hw=20 validation script (Final/goldbach_stats_hw20.py). Earlier scripts in Historical/ reflect development stages associated with narrower arc neighbourhoods and are retained for reproducibility of intermediate analyses documented in §5.3 and Appendix C of the paper.
The initial correlation r ≈ 0.83 at hw=2 was revised to r ≈ 0.67 at hw=20 after identifying the aperture amplification artefact. This correction is documented in the paper.
Hardware
All computations were performed on a single workstation: Ubuntu, 48GB RAM, 16 threads, NVMe SSD. The 10^9 scale requires approximately 80 minutes per scan window using the Goertzel method with 12 parallel workers.
Reproducibility
All computations are deterministic. Output checksums are recorded in the cached JSON files. Cross-validation between FFT and Goertzel methods at overlapping scales agrees to relative error below 10^-7.
